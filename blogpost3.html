Some small changes that reduce your carbon footprint

A lasting and flourishing planet requires work from each and every one of us. As responsible consumers, every little lifestyle change we make adds up to create a significant impact on our environment. Here are some small but significant everyday changes you can make:

  1. Consider renewable sources of energy. Granted, they can be slightly more expensive per unit consumed, it is a small cost that goes towards significantly reducing your carbon consumption.
  2. Opt for flights with low carbon emissions and eco-certified hotels when travelling.
  3. Choose public transport, cycling and electric cars over conventional gas vehicles as much as possible.
  4. Switch to LED bulbs. These consume less electricity and last longer than traditional incandescent bulbs.
  5. Opt for slow fashion brands. Also give your clothes another chance by donating them or taking part in clothes swaps.
  6. Shop at thrift stores for some great vintage pieces and give these unique finds a new life in your closet.
  7. Pick locally produced fruits and veggies. This produce travels a shorter distance to reach your neighbourhood stores and thus has a lower carbon footprint.
